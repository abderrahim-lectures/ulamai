\section{Group Theory: Sylow's First Theorem}

\begin{theorem}[sylow\_existence]
Let $G$ be a finite group with $|G| = p^n m$, where $p$ is prime and $p$ does
not divide $m$. Then $G$ has a subgroup of order $p^n$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By induction on $|G|$. If $G$ is abelian, use Cauchy's theorem repeatedly on
the quotient by a central subgroup of order $p$. In general, if $p \mid |Z(G)|$,
Cauchy's theorem gives a central subgroup $N$ of order $p$; by induction
$G/N$ has a subgroup of order $p^{n-1}$, whose preimage in $G$ has order $p^n$.
Otherwise $p \nmid |Z(G)|$, so by the class equation
$|G| = |Z(G)| + \sum_i [G : C_G(x_i)]$ some conjugacy class term
$[G : C_G(x_i)]$ is coprime to $p$, so $p^n \mid |C_G(x_i)|$ for a
proper subgroup $C_G(x_i) \subsetneq G$; by induction $C_G(x_i)$ has a
subgroup of order $p^n$, which is the desired subgroup of $G$.
\end{proof}
