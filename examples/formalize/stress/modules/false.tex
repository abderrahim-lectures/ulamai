\section{Module Theory}

\begin{theorem}[module\_over\_pid\_is\_free]
Every module $M$ over a principal ideal domain $R$ is a free $R$-module.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By the structure theorem for modules over a PID, every module decomposes
as a direct sum of a free part and cyclic torsion parts $R/(d_i)$; taking
each $d_i$ to be a generator of the corresponding ideal, $R/(d_i) \cong R$
as $R$-modules, so every summand is free and hence $M$ is free.
\end{proof}
